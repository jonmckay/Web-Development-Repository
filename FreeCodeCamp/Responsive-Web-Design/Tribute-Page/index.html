Tribute Page

Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs shows off the iPhone 4 at the 2010 Worldwide Developers Conference.
  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco
  • 1961 - Moves to Mountain View, California (later known as Silicon Valley)
  • 1968 - Gets a summer internship at Hewlett-Packard by calling and asking Bill Hewlett for frequency counter parts.
  • 1970 - Meets future Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak through a friend
  • 1972 - Graduates from Homestead High School in Cupertino, California
  • 1974 - Begins a brief stint as an engineer at Atari
  • 1976 - Co-funds Apple Computer with Wozniak and Ronald Wayne
  • 1977 - Apple incorporates
  • 1977 - Releases the Apple II
  • 1980 - Apple goes public
  • 1985 - CEO John Sculley engineers Jobs' ouster from Apple. Jobs resigns as Apple chairman.
  • 1986 - Buys the Graphics Group division of Lucasfilm that becomes Pixar Animation Studios for $10 million.
  • 1988 - NeXT Computer releases its first computer.
  • 1993 - NeXT discontinues hardware business, gets into software instead. The company is renamed NeXT Software, Inc.
  • 1995 - Becomes Pixar's president and CEO. Later in the year, Jobs brings Pixar public one week after the release of "Toy Story."
  • 1996 - Returns to Apple, as an adviser, after it buys NeXT for $429 million.
  • 1997 - Becomes CEO, initially as the de facto chief, then as interim chief in September.
  • 1997 - Introduces the Apple Store, which lets consumers custom-order Apple products directly from the company online.
  • 1998 - Apple returns to profitability.
  • 1998 - Introduces the iMac, which becomes commercially available in August.
  • 2000 - Becomes CEO of Apple again
  • 2001 - Introduces ITunes exclusively for Mac users.
  • 2002 - Apple introduces the first Windows-compatible iPods at Macworld.
  • 2003 - Opens the ITunes store to the United States.
  • 2003 - Introduces iTunes' compatibility with Windows.
  • 2004 - Jobs undergoes surgery to remove a cancerous tumor in his pancreas.
  • 2005 - Apple introduces an iPod Shuffle.
  • 2005 - Announces switch from IBM chips to Intel's Core Duo chips with Apple's new MacBook Pro and iMac.
  • 2005 - Introduces iPod Nano.
  • 2005 - Introduces iPod with video.
  • 2006 - Apples market capitalization surpasses Dell's
  • 2006 - Disney buys Pixar for $7.4 billion with Jobs on Disney's board as its top shareholder.
  • 2006 - iTunes sells its billionth song.
  • 2007 - Drops "Computer" from Apple's name. Introduces the iPhone.
  • 2007 - Introduces the iPod Touch
  • 2007 - Inducted in the California Hall of Fame by Gov. Arnold Schwartzenegger.
  • 2008 - Introduces the App Store as an update to iTunes. Also introduces the MacBook Air.
  • 2008 - Unveils MobileMe, Apple's first cloud offering.
  • 2009 - Takes a 6-month leave of absence for medical reasons.
  • 2010 - Introduces the iPad.
  • 2011 - Apple starts selling iPhones via Verizon Wireless.
  • 2011 - Gives a keynote speech at Apple's Worldwide Developers Conference, it's Jobs' last public appearance at an Apple event.
  • 2011 - Pitches Cupertino on a new Apple campus.
  • 2011 - Apple shares edge past those of Exxon Mobil to become the most valuable U.S. company, in terms of market capitalization, at $337.17 billion.
  • 2011 - Resigns from Apple CEO post; becomes chairman.
